About Me

Passionate Biochemist & Researcher

Hello! I'm Mahedi Hassan, a dedicated biochemist from Dhaka, Bangladesh, holding a B.Sc. in Biochemistry and Molecular Biology from Gono Bishwabidyalay.

My academic journey combines experience as a lecturer in medical institutes with a growing research focus on computational drug discovery. I am currently engaged in in-silico research, employing molecular docking, molecular dynamics simulations, and AI-assisted tools to investigate natural compounds such as curcumin and methoxsalen derivatives as potential therapeutic candidates.

My research interests lie at the interface of molecular biology, chemistry, and computational modeling, where predictive simulations can accelerate and refine drug development strategies.

I strongly believe that the future of biomedical research lies in the integration of wet-lab experimentation and computational innovation. Beyond academic work, I am an avid reader and advocate for science education and public health awareness.

Publications

Effects of Supplementing Layers with Varying Levels of Alfalfa and Moringa Fresh Leaves on Egg Production and Quality

Alemayehu Guteta, Mahedi Hassan, Khokon Hossain, Mammo Mengesha Erdaw

Poultry Science Research | December 2023

Abstract: This feeding trial was conducted to evaluate and demonstrate the effects of supplementing intensively managed commercial layer chickens with varying levels of fresh leaves of Medicago sativa (Alfalfa) and Moringa stenopetala on the characteristics of eggs. A 3x3 factorial arrangement was used with levels of Alfalfa fresh leaves at 0, 200, or 400 g.

Identification, Pathogenicity, Public Health and Economic Importance of Tapeworms of Poultry

Daniel Tamirat, Mahedi Hassan, Saima Akter Bristy, Md Khokon Hossain, Md. Raquibul Islam

Veterinary Parasitology | February 2024

Abstract: Tapeworms of poultry, prevalent in Ethiopia and other tropical regions, pose significant economic and health challenges. They parasitize the intestinal tract of birds, leading to reduced production, growth retardation, weight loss, and mortality. Common genera include Davainea, Hymenolepis, Amoebotaenia, Choanotaenia, and Raillietina, with complex life cycles requiring intermediate hosts.
